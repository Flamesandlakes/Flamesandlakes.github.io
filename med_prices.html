What is the price of medicine?

As both the price of medicinal products and the products themselves change over time, I will start by looking at price based on the average price of the product across its time on the market.
This allows me to better compare products that may not otherwise appear on the market in the same time frames.

For some descriptive statistics, the range of average sales prices ranges from 0.05 DKK per DDD to as high as 2,370,000 DKK per DDD in some outlier cases.

The mean of the price range is 732.16 DKK per DDD whereas the median lies at just 12.88 DKK per DDD.

I will first add that the mean price of individual products does not necessarily reflect medicine costs for the average consumer as it does not factor in weights based on how widely used the given product is.

The sudden increase in mean price in October of 2020 can be attributed to the introduction of Idefirix to the market alongside a range of other highly priced products (albeit not as expensive as Idefirix).



So what does the development in price look like when we remove such outliers?



Outliers are defined based on their average prize across the entire time period. The outliers are thus treated as the same products regardless of the time window.

Averages can be a good indicator at times by informing about the broader picture. In this case, they tell us that we can expect the sale prices of pharmaceutical products to generally increase as time progresses. But as a metric it is also susceptible to be influenced by outliers.

An average based all individual products leads us to lose some details. There are many variables that may play into pricing where the data gives us access to some. Of particular interest to me is the ATC codes.

How long does products stay on market?

The number of products on market does not remain constant across time.

There were 7108 medical producs (with a corresponding DDD) listed on the 7th of May 2018.
On the 6th of June 2023, that number sit at 7197.

But the graph below reveals that the development is more than a simple increase, as products enter and exit from the pharmacy listing at different rates throughout the time period.


NOTE: ADD axis labels + reformat dates

In terms of how long products stay on the market, 26% of medical products are observed at all biweekly time windows for a minimum of 1876 days - a bit more than 5 years.
The average time on market sits on 1154 days while the median sits at 1236 days. As a point of reference, 3 years is equivalent to 1095 days.
The mean and the median are both dragged upwards by the aforementioned 26% of medical products. The time on market among products are otherwise fairly evenly distributed when you look aside products extending beyond the observed time period.
